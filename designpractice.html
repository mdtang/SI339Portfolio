Practicing for Interview Design Exercise

Applying for Design Internships

Starting in September 2017, I began to search and apply for User Experience Design and Product Design internships, mainly in Silicon Valley and the Seattle-Redmond area.

For three months, I received no interview offers. I was very worried, but throughout this time, I periodically worked on improving my portfolio and began working as a User Experience intern at the local DaySmart Software with Tonya McCarley.

Preparing for My Interviews

Luckily, I received several internship interview offers in January. Some of them were:

  • Amazon
  • Bloomberg
  • Paypal
  • Blizzard

I was aware that I had to prepare for two essential aspects of a design interview. Portfolio walkthrough/presentation and design challenge/exercise.

The Amazon internship was the one that I wanted to pass the most, so I studied the 14 Amazon Leadership Principles and tried to incorporate them into my portfolio review.

Fo the design exercise, I went to a study room in North Quad and attempted a few design challenges, including an app for sharing items. I kept the topic of the exercise vague, just like how it would be during the actual interview, to practice my skills in asking questions and coming up with ideas on the fly.
